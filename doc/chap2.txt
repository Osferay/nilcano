  [1X2.1 [33X[0;0YCanonical conjugate representatives for elements[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YSuppose that the finitely generated group [23XG[123X is given by a nilpotent sequence
  [23X(g_1,\dots,g_n)[123X. For [23Xg \in G [123X and [23XU \leq G[123X, we denote the conjugacy class of
  [23Xg[123X  under [23XU[123X by [23Xg^U = {g^h | h \in U}[123X and the centralizer of [23Xg[123X in [23XU[123X by [23XC_U (g)
  = \{h \in U | g^h = g\}[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet  [23X\ll[123X  be  the well-order [23X0 \ll 1 \ll 2 \ll \dots \ll −1 \ll −2 \dots [123X of
  [23X\mathbb{Z}[123X.   We   order  the  exponent  vectors  extending  the  well-order
  lexicographically; that is, [23X(e_1,\dots, e_n) \ll (f_1, \dots, f_n)[123X if [23X e_1 =
  f_1,  \dots , e_{i−1} = f_{i−1}[123X and [23Xe_i \ll f_i[123X for some [23Xi \in {1,\dots,n} [123X.
  We  order  the elements of G via their exponent vectors; that is, [23Xg \ll h[123X if
  [23Xe(g)  \leq  e(h)[123X.  One of the main program of this package is to compute the
  minimum  element  in  [23X g^U [123X with respect to [23X\ll[123X this element is known as the
  canonical  conjugacy  representative  of  [23X  g[123X,  this  element  is denoted as
  [23XCano_U(g)[123X.  This program also computes the centralizer [23X C_U(g)[123X. This package
  is supplementary to the article of Eick and Fernández Ayala [EFA25].[133X

  [1X2.2 [33X[0;0YCanonical conjugate representatives for subgroups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YSuppose that the finitely generated group [23XG[123X is given by a nilpotent sequence
  [23X(g_1,\dots,g_n)[123X.  Given  [23XU, V \leq G[123X we write [23XU^V = \{U^g | g ∈ V \}[123X for the
  conjugacy  class  of  [23XU[123X  under  [23XV[123X and [23XN_V(U) = \{g \in V | U^g = U\}[123X for the
  corresponding  normalizer.  Let  [23XU  \leq G[123X be given by its basis [23X(u_1,\dots,
  u_n)[123X.  Let  [23XN  \leq  N_G(U)[123X and [23Xg \in N[123X. We define [23XCano_N^U(g)[123X as the unique
  reduced   preimage  of  [23XCano_{N/U}(gU)[123X  under  the  natural  homomorphism  [23XN
  \rightarrow N/U[123X. Now we consider two subgroups [23XW[123X and [23XV[123X of [23XG[123X, and write [23XW_i =
  W  \cap G_i [123X for [23X1 \leq i \leq n[123X. We define [23XCano_V (W )[123X inductively: suppose
  that  [23XU_{i+1}  =  Cano_V  (W_i+1)[123X  is  given  by  a basis [23Xu_{i+1},\dots, u_n[123X
  together with a conjugating element [23XU_i+1 = W^v_{i+1}[123X and its normalizer [23XN =
  N_V  (U_{i+1})[123X. Suppose that [23XW_i \neq W_{i+1}[123X and [23XW_i = ⟨w_i, W_{i+1}⟩ [123X. Let
  [23Xw_i^\prime[123X  be  the  normalized  power  of  the  conjugate  [23Xw^v_i[123X. Set [23Xu_i =
  Cano^{U_{i+1}}_N(w^\prime_i)[123X,  then  [23X(u_i,\dots,  u_n)[123X  is  a  basis  for  a
  subgroup  [23XU_i[123X  of  [23XG_i[123X. We write [23XCano_V (W )[123X for the subgroup [23XU_1[123X eventually
  determined  by  an iterated process. One of the main program of this package
  is  to  compute  [23XCano_V  (W )[123X. This program also computes the normalizer of [23X
  N_V(W) [123X.[133X
